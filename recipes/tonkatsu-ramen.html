Tonkatsu Ramen Recipe

Description:

Tonkatsu ramen is a rich and flavorful Japanese noodle soup, celebrated for its creamy, pork-based broth. The broth, made by simmering pork bones for hours, achieves a velvety texture and deep umami flavor that forms the heart of the dish. Thin, firm noodles are paired with this luscious broth, creating a satisfying base for an array of toppings that add layers of texture and taste. Traditional garnishes include tender slices of chashu (braised pork belly), a marinated soft-boiled egg with a creamy yolk, and crisp vegetables like green onions and bamboo shoots.

The balance of flavors in tonkatsu ramen makes it a favorite comfort food. The rich, savory broth is complemented by the sweetness of corn, the spice of chili oil, or the freshness of nori (seaweed). The result is a bowl that combines warmth and indulgence, offering a sensory experience that captures the essence of Japanese culinary craftsmanship.

Ingredients:

For the broth:

  • Pork bones (neck or femur bones, thoroughly cleaned)
  • Water
  • Garlic (whole cloves, optional)
  • Ginger (sliced)
  • Green onions (whole or cut into large pieces)
  • Onion (halved)
  • Kombu (dried kelp, optional for additional umami)
  • Shiitake mushrooms (optional for added depth)

For the toppings:

  • Chashu (braised pork belly or shoulder)
  • Marinated soft-boiled eggs (ajitsuke tamago)
  • Green onions (sliced)
  • Bamboo shoots (menma)
  • Nori (seaweek sheets)
  • Bean sprouts (optional)
  • Corn (optional)
  • Chili oil or spicy miso paste (optional)

For the noodles:

  • Fresh ramen noodles

For seasoning the broth:

  • Soy sauce
  • Miso paste (optional)
  • Salt
  • Sesame oil

Steps:

Prepare the broth

  1. Clean the pork bones: Rinse the pork bones under cold water, then boil them for 10 minutes. Discard the water and scrub the bones to remove impurities. This step ensures a clear, clean broth.
  2. Simmer the bones: Place the cleaned bones in a large pot with water. Add garlic, ginger, onion, green onions, and optional kombu or shiitake mushrooms. Simmer on low heat for 10â€“12 hours, skimming off impurities occasionally to keep the broth creamy and smooth.
  4. Strain the broth: Once cooked, strain the broth to remove solids, leaving only the rich, creamy liquid. Season with soy sauce, miso paste, salt, or sesame oil to taste.

Prepare the toppings

  1. Make the chashu: Braise pork belly or shoulder in a mixture of soy sauce, sake, sugar, and water until tender. Slice thinly for topping.
  2. Marinate the eggs: Soft-boil eggs, peel them, and marinate in soy sauce, mirin, and water for several hours to develop flavor.
  3. Prepare other toppings: Slice green onions, bamboo shoots, and nori. Cook optional vegetables like bean sprouts or corn.

Cook the noodles

  1. Boil ramen noodles: Cook fresh ramen noodles in boiling water according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.

Assemble the ramen

  • Build the bowl: Place cooked noodles in a bowl. Ladle the hot broth over the noodles.
  • Add toppings: Arrange slices of chashu, marinated egg halves, green onions, bamboo shoots, nori, and other toppings on top of the noodles. Add chili oil or spicy miso paste if desired.
  • Serve: Serve immediately while piping hot and enjoy the rich, comforting flavors of homemade tonkotsu ramen.
